# CRUCIBLE — The Forge Wake

*Ore selected 2026-07-08 via `/recombine` on the skill portfolio (pass-2 fold), forged through `/crucible`.*

## The ore (the pumped case)

**The Forge Wake** — the repo proposes its own highest-value work at the start of every session, ranked by **aliveness × impact × continuity-with-your-last-thread**, so you never sit down to a blank "what should I work on?" or a flat, unranked restored-task dump.

It's the third thing born between `/consolidate` (looks *backward*: session → durable memory + `next-session-prompt.md`) and `/crucible --scout` (looks *forward*: repo → ranked ore). They already touch the same object — the session boundary — from opposite directions. Fuse them there and you get a ranking neither parent could produce: `/consolidate` knows what you *cared about* but can't rank buildable ore; `/crucible` ranks ore but doesn't know what excited you last night. The child does both.

## Why this one (aliveness × impact)

- **Aliveness 3** — *oh, of course* the repo should greet you with its own ranked ambitions. First-hand evidence, not a vibe: **this very session opened with 8 restored tasks as a flat, unranked list** (`project:claude-skills`), and I had to eyeball which mattered. The Forge Wake is the fix for a step I watched fail today.
- **Impact 3** — fires at *every* session start, for *every* project. Removes a real recurring decision (what to build) and the flat-backlog problem. Design-for-subtraction: it deletes a human task rather than adding an AI-mediated one.

## The falsifier (the one thing that would prove this slag)

If `/crucible --scout`'s ranking is **noisy or untrustworthy in practice**, an auto-briefing at every wake becomes noise you learn to ignore — strictly worse than a blank start. The whole thing rests on the scout's ranking being *stable and trustworthy enough* that reading it is worth the two seconds. Heat must probe this before wiring it to every session.

## Consent spine

Read-only by construction (`--scout` forges nothing; it *proposes*). The forge it might trigger still passes `/crucible`'s own consent gate. Blast-radius: a briefing, not a build.
