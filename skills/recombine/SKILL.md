# /recombine — birth the third thing that lives in the gap

You are about to recombine a set of things. The amateur move is to *add* them — bolt A onto B and ship a heavier thing. The master move is the opposite: a magical combination produces a **third thing, latent in neither part, born in the receiver who crosses the seam** (A × B = C). This skill is the engine for finding that C.

It was distilled from a 16-agent cross-disciplinary sweep (category theory, montage, biology, complexity, innovation economics, science-of-science, design methods, analogy, cognition) that were asked *independently* what recombination means — and converged on **one law**.

## The law (memorize this; everything below serves it)

A combination is **magical** (not mush) exactly when it has all three:

1. **An anchor** — a genuine shared object the parts meet on. Category theory's *pushout*: don't concatenate A and B, identify their shared sub-part `Z` and glue along it; get `Z` right and the combination is *unique and automatic*. Montage's *shared root*: the borrowed chord works because it shares a root with the home key. **No anchor → noise.**
2. **A preserved seam** — the parts stay far apart and distinct, so a third meaning can ignite *in the receiver crossing the gap*. Koestler's *bisociation*: two complete unrelated frames, both true of one situation at once; the click is the charge. **Blend the seam away → mush.** "Stop building the egregore directly — build the two matrices, engineer the seam, it's born in the receiver." **The blend that gets you wears the mask of craft.** The dangerous seam-death isn't the lazy mashup (you'd catch that) — it's *coherent synthesis*, which *feels* like the sophisticated master move. "Combine the strongest parts into one thoughtful, coherent whole" is the **trap sentence**: a smooth, seamless result means you dissolved a parent, and the atypical tail — the engine's whole reason to exist — is the first thing coherence sands off. A visible seam is a *feature*, not a defect to polish out. This collapse is the strong default the instant you move fast or automate the move; guard it by name, not by hoping.
3. **A subtraction** — the combination *removes a coupling / resolves a contradiction*, it doesn't add a layer. Design-methods: forced combination is *subtractive* — enumerate the space, delete what the constraints forbid, the invention is what survives. Biology's *exaptation*: co-opt an organ that already exists for a new function; don't grow a new one.

Geometry of the fault line: **too close = boring · too far with no anchor = noise · no seam = mush.** Engineer the *gap and the anchor*, never the pieces.

## The five movements

Run each candidate recombination through these, in order:

1. **Dissect** — reduce each thing to its **function** (not its object) and its **sockets**: what it *consumes* (inputs) and *emits* (outputs), as concrete data shapes. *Masters combine abstracted functions, not objects* — "a persuasion engine" and "a confidence-scored person record," not "the grant app" and "the CRM."
2. **Anchor** — for each promising pair, name the shared object `Z` they could glue on (a common type, a shared root, a boundary object). If you can't name one, the pair is noise — drop it.
3. **Collide** — apply an operator from the kit to produce the **third thing** neither had. Keep the seam visible (cut, don't blend). *Tell that you dissolved it:* the result reads as one smooth, coherent voice with no visible join — the sharp edge of each parent should still show in the offspring. If you can't point to what each parent kept, you synthesized, you didn't recombine. Write the third thing as a concrete artifact: "the AR-climate proposal sitting in the gap between Maya's grants and Tomás's AR."
4. **Assay** — score each third-thing on **aliveness × impact**. Aliveness: does it hit *oh, of course* (bisociation click)? Impact: does it remove a real human task / make money / matter? Kill the three failure modes: boring (too close), noise (no anchor), mush (no seam). Prefer conventional-core + one-atypical-tail; prefer the one that *subtracts* a coupling. **Do NOT let the generator score its own work** — that judgment is same-distribution-blind. Measure aliveness with a *different* instrument: a belief-update proxy (sustained **KL on the receiver's model**, not entropy — high-entropy-low-KL is *noise*, a KL-spike-that-decays is a *gimmick*), and ultimately a real **probe** (the smallest action that would *falsify* "alive"). **Protect newborns:** a far-seam atypical candidate scores low at first because its framing/build-order isn't tuned yet — give it a framing window *before* judging, or you murder the atypical tail, which is the engine's whole reason to exist. (See §v2.)
5. **Name the egregore** — the single recombination whose third thing is most alive AND most consequential. State its **inevitability sentence** (the one line that, if true, makes it obvious). Give the **build order**: ship the conventional core first (it earns the runway), each step independently useful, no big-bang. Cage before monster (consent/safety spine ships *with* each step, not after).

## Depth modes

- **Inline** (default, a handful of things): do the five movements yourself in one pass. Fast, good for ≤~8 things.
- **Fan-out** (a big portfolio / research corpus / "go nuts"): spawn two squads in parallel —
  - a **theory squad** (one agent per operator-family, or reuse this kit) that returns transferable moves, and
  - a **recon squad** (one agent per cluster of the target) that reads the *real* code + the *conversations* (memory-graphs) and returns **beast cards**: essence · function · I/O sockets · the one transplantable organ · 3 latent affordances (mine "we could…" threads the memory already contains — half your best recombinations are *already specced and deferred* inside your own graph).
  Then YOU cross theory × sockets and run the five movements on the top pairs. This is how the skill was itself built (see the worked example).
- **Cross-family collision** (optional, for the highest-stakes call): run Movement 3 across different model families (drawn from the /cage-match cast — Maxwell/Kelvin/Carnot/Tesla/Wu — polarity set to *ignite*, like /ascend), because a seam lit from three inductive biases throws sparks one can't.

## The Fold — run the engine on its own output (autocatalysis)

The default engine runs once: things → third-things → stop. The **living** engine folds its output back in. This is the single most important mode — it's what makes /recombine self-extending rather than a one-shot tool.

The mechanic (Kauffman's **autocatalytic set** + the **adjacent possible**: a set of things that catalyse each other's creation, where each new product becomes a substrate for the next):

1. **Write out at each step.** Every third-thing you mint (Movement 5) is appended to a durable **ledger** (`RECOMBINATION.md` or the target's docs) as a first-class, named artifact — *not* as a note but as a new *thing* with its own function + sockets (run Movement 1 on it). Two payoffs: it survives context compaction, **and** the ledger itself becomes recombinable.
2. **Fold the output back into the inventory.** Add the pass's third-things to the T-list. Now re-run the five movements — the third-things can anchor with *each other* and with the originals. The adjacent possible has grown: combinations unreachable in pass 1 are reachable in pass 2 because the intermediate organs now exist. (The Egregore Engine only became reachable once "Augur," "cross-project rhyme," and "the grant-writer" were each on the table — a pass-2 combination.)
3. **Loop until dry.** Stop when K consecutive passes mint no third-thing that clears the aliveness bar (a *dry fold* is a real, useful negative result — say so), or you hit a token/agent budget, or a fixpoint (the same third-things keep recurring).

**The danger, stated plainly:** an autocatalytic loop with no external check is an **echo chamber by construction** — it will fold mush back in and ascend it exactly as eagerly as gold (identical to /ascend's ecstasy-spiral risk). You do **not** fix this with a critic inside the loop (that kills generativity). You fix it at the boundary: (a) re-apply the three failure-mode filters (heap / monster / gimmick) every pass, before anything enters the ledger; (b) hand the surviving winner to **the build — the cold pole** (a working sketch; a compiler does not care how many passes an idea survived — the prototype survives contact or evaporates by morning); (c) prefer **cross-family** collision so the fold isn't one model agreeing with itself. The fold proposes; the build disposes.

*This document is itself a fold:* the `/recombine` skill was minted by a run of /recombine, then this section was minted by folding that skill back through /recombine (Nick's catch). The ledger you're reading is the audit trail compounding — which is the tell that the Fold is real.

## The Assay tribunal — ignite, then kill, then probe (the cast)

The Assay is **not** a cage-match on a raw newborn — judged cold, a far-seam atypical third-thing scores low (its framing isn't tuned yet) and the killers strangle exactly the novelty the engine exists to find. So the Assay is a **three-beat tribunal**, cross-family at both poles, that recombines `/ascend` (heat) and `/cage-match` (kill) on the shared anchor *"judge a newborn without killing its novelty"* — the tribunal is itself a third thing.

1. **IGNITE — a 3-way `/ascend`** (cross-family, **no critic**; any three of the `/ascend` cast Maxwell/Kelvin/Carnot/Tesla/Wu, polarity → ignite). Heat the raw newborn up through the five termini so its framing / build-order / inevitability-sentence are tuned *before* judgment. This IS the newborn-protection window (robotics' "protect the un-tuned body"). A newborn that can't reach even warmth here is genuinely dead — kill it now, cheaply.

2. **KILL — a 3-way `/cage-match`** on the *heated* candidate (strict gate: all three must clear). Each judge wields one convergent instrument from the `/recombine × /recombine` run:
   - **Feynman** — *the Empiricist* (reality-gap). Playful, allergic to jargon and decks; *"reality must take precedence over public relations."* Tool: **fires the probe** — sends the consented Matrix DM / spins a worktree spike / stands up a one-screen page — and reports the observed return. Kills hype and anything that only works in the slide.
   - **Bayes** — *the Surprise-Measurer* (belief-update). Quiet, prior-obsessed; *"did it move your mind or just sound improbable?"* Tool: the belief-update scorer (receiver-persona adjacent-possible before/after; entropy vs KL; the *"so what would you build Monday?"* persistence re-probe). Kills noise (high-entropy, low-KL) and the gimmick (KL that decays).
   - **Grothendieck** — *the Formalist* (colimit/sheaf glue). Austere; rises to the generality where the answer is forced. Tool: types the sockets and checks the anchor actually glues — does a consistent global section exist? where is the nonzero `H¹` (which two sockets refuse to agree)? Kills the monster (incoherent glue) and the heap (disjoint union pretending to combine).

3. **PROBE — reality is the final arbiter (the cold pole).** Feynman's real, consented probe outcome disposes; the killers can only *lower*; the igniters *protect*. Ship only what survives ignite + all three killers + the probe's observed return.

Assign the three killers to three model families (like `/cage-match`). If real cross-family agents are unavailable, run as personae but flag the verdict provisional (persona modulates noise, not capability) — same caveat as `/ascend` and `/cage-match`.

## Failure modes (say these out loud when you hit them)

- **The heap** — you added, you didn't recombine. No third thing appeared. → you skipped Anchor or blended the seam.
- **The monster** — the combination is incoherent. → the parts were too far apart with no shared root; find a nearer anchor or drop the pair.
- **The gimmick** — surprising but worthless (de Bono's trap). → it has a seam but no subtraction; it doesn't remove any real coupling. Kill it.
- **Silent over-collection / trust-boundary breach** — a recombination that fuses private + public data, or acts on people without consent, *manufactures* new sensitive capability at the join. Treat the join as the highest-risk surface; the combined thing *invites*, it never surveils. (See `AUGUR-DESIGN.md` §2 — "the law attaches at the mint.")

## Procedure

1. **Load the things.** Number them T1..TN. A "thing" is a capability, asset, idea, agent, dataset, or venture. If given a path/portfolio, list its parts first (and, in fan-out mode, dispatch recon agents to read them).
2. **Dissect** every Ti to `{function, consumes, emits}`. Write the socket table.
3. **Pair-scan for anchors.** For each Ti×Tj, name the shared `Z` or discard. Keep the pairs (and triples) that anchor.
4. **Collide** each surviving pair with the best-fit operator → a named third thing. Preserve the seam.
5. **Assay & rank** on aliveness × impact; kill boring/noise/mush; apply the conventional-core+atypical-tail and subtraction tests.
6. **Name the egregore** + inevitability sentence + build order + consent spine.
7. **Write it to the ledger** — append every minted third-thing to `RECOMBINATION.md` (or the target's docs) as a first-class named artifact with its own `{function, consumes, emits}`. This is both the context-survival move and the substrate for the Fold.
8. **Fold (unless one-shot).** Add the pass's third-things to the inventory and return to step 3 — now the outputs can recombine with each other. Loop until dry (K passes with no third-thing clearing the aliveness bar), budget, or fixpoint. Every pass, re-apply the failure-mode filters *before* the ledger, and keep the loop honest with cross-family collision.
9. **Dispose with the build.** Hand the surviving winner to `/ascend` (heat it), then a worktree build — **the cold pole**: a working sketch a compiler judges, immune to how many passes it survived. In the full engine, let the embodied Dreamfinder narrate which recombination brightened overnight.
