# /crucible — melt an idea from ore to a tempered blade

You are about to forge something. A crucible is a vessel that survives extreme heat so the metal inside can melt, separate from its slag, take a mold, and come out hard. This skill is that vessel for an *idea*: it starts with raw enthusiasm (heat) and ends with a plan you can start on Monday (a blade). Unlike its sibling engines, **you do not wait for the topic** — `/crucible` roams the codebase and the open task list, develops taste, and *chooses the most alive candidate itself*, then drives it all the way to a vetted plan.

## The law (everything below serves it)

**Heat without a mold is a puddle; a mold without heat is empty.** Enthusiasm is the *fuel*, never the product. `/crucible` earns the right to get genuinely, unironically pumped about an idea only because a **mold** (the design doc) and a **hammer** (the cage-match) are waiting downstream to give the heat form and beat the brittleness out. The joy is real and load-bearing — it's what makes the scout pick something worth building instead of something safe — but it is disposed of by the temper, in the same pass, before any plan is trusted.

This is the family's third thermodynamic position:

> **/ascend** heats with NO critic and defers the cold pole to an overnight build (hot → matter). **/spiral-review** cools a live finding down to a function-on-data (hot → contract). **/crucible** heats to *select*, pours into a mold, and tempers in the SAME pass — ending in a **plan**, before a line of code (hot → blueprint).

`/ascend`'s honesty-restorer is a compiler tomorrow. `/crucible`'s is the cage-match at movement 4 — the adversary is built into the middle, not deferred. That is the whole reason this is a distinct engine and not "`/ascend` with extra steps."

## The five movements (metallurgy, in order)

1. **Ore** — *scout two ways, then select on aliveness × impact.* Roam the real substrate: `grep`/read the codebase, and read the **open task list** (`gh issue list -R nickmeinhold/claude-tasks --label "project:<slug>" --state open`, plus the in-session TaskList) and carried-forward `project_*_forward_plan.md` "crazy-ideas" threads. The scout runs **two scan passes and picks the single winner across both** — a candidate is not only a new capability:
   - **Forward scan** — *what could we build?* New features, capabilities, ideas the substrate is reaching for.
   - **Inward scan** — *what should we rework?* Opportunities to **extract** (duplication → a shared module), **combine** (two things that should be one — dispatch to `/recombine`), **simplify** (a tangle that should collapse — dispatch to `/simplify`), or **redesign** (a subsystem that's rotting and wants a new shape). Smell-detect: duplication clusters, tight coupling, complexity hotspots, drift, N-similar-artifacts, dead code.

   Crucible does not *replace* `/simplify` or `/recombine` — its scout autonomously *finds* the opportunity none of them scout for, and Cast/Heat may **call them as operators** (as it composes `/deep-research` and `/cage-match`). Sometimes the highest-impact ore in a codebase is a redesign, not a new feature — a scout blind to that would lie about optimizing for impact. Surface a handful of candidates across BOTH scans, then **reject the slag** — the dull, the already-done, and the thrilling-but-trivial — and keep the ONE that **glows AND matters most**. Selection is the *meet of two axes, not one*: **aliveness** (does it hit *oh, of course* — the thing you'd want to build) × **impact** (does it remove a real human task, unblock something stuck, matter to a user or to Nick). Highest impact alone is the wrong knob — it's a pure argmax that sands off the atypical tail (a genuinely novel idea scores low on impact *at first*, precisely because it's new); highest glow alone picks a shiny toy that fixes nothing. Take the candidate at the **peak of the product**, and when two are close, break the tie toward **impact** (the whole point is to forge something that changes the world a little, not just something fun). Then **get pumped**: state plainly and with real heat *why this one* — what makes it thrilling AND what it would actually change (the human task it removes, the thing it unblocks) — and the one-line spark that, if true, makes you want to drop everything. This is the joy beat; do not flatten it into a neutral status report. But **keep the heat separate from the evidence, and name the falsifier**: alongside the spark, state the one thing that, if true, would prove this ore is slag (persuasive affect is not a substitute for a claim that could be wrong — Carnot efficiency is useful work extracted between reservoirs, not maximum temperature). **Verify the ore is real** — the candidate must point at an artifact that exists (a file, an issue, a live thread), not an invention (verify-before-asserting; the scout selects from what IS, never from what would be cool if it existed).

2. **Heat** — *melt it down.* Run a deep research pass on the chosen candidate to separate metal from slag: prior art, existing implementations in this repo and the wider world, the real constraints, the failure modes others already hit, the libraries/APIs/patterns that apply. Compose with **`/deep-research`** (or a background `general-purpose` researcher) — fan out, fetch sources, verify claims, and write findings to `RESEARCH.md` in the target's dir. Heat is where "wouldn't it be cool" meets the ground truth of what's actually known.

3. **Cast** — *give it shape.* Pour the melt into a mold: write a **design doc** (`DESIGN.md`, placed per the *Contracts & guardrails* rules below). It states the problem, the proposed shape (architecture, interfaces, data shapes, the one atypical element), the **build order** (conventional core first, each step independently useful, no big-bang), the tradeoffs taken, and — because this is a Nick-shaped forge — the **blast-radius and consent spine up front** (cage before monster: name the owner, the injection surface, the throttle *in the design*, not as a follow-up). Crucially, Cast carries two things forward for the adversary to strike: a **"claims to falsify" section** (the enthusiasm's load-bearing assumptions, stated as things that could be *wrong*) and the **rejected alternatives** (what simpler shape you passed over, and why). This is what stops the excited author from laundering the design into something un-attackable. A design with `[TODO]`/`[CONFIRM]` gaps self-reports as unfinished — enumerate the open variables explicitly rather than rounding to "ready."

4. **Temper** — *harden it under fire.* Tempering removes brittleness by cycling heat and stress; the design cage-match does the same. Run **`/cage-match` on the design** (not code) with the cross-family cast (Maxwell/Kelvin/Carnot/Tesla) set to *hunt fatal design flaws*: unstated assumptions, missing failure modes, a simpler alternative that dissolves the problem, a wrong option-frame, the blast-radius the Cast under-counted, the coupling that should be removed rather than guarded. **Feed the adversary all three hot-phase artifacts — `CRUCIBLE.md` (the enthusiasm case), `RESEARCH.md`, and `DESIGN.md` — not just the polished design**, because irreversibility enters *before* the cold sink: the design may already have laundered assumptions the enthusiasm smuggled in, and the adversary can only catch that if it sees the enthusiasm. **Strict gate:** every finding is either folded back into the design (return to Cast, re-temper) or explicitly recorded as a **named tradeoff** (owner + accepted cost + mitigation-or-abandonment rationale — a finding papered over as "acceptable for now" with no owner is not a tradeoff, it's a silent liability). **The loop is bounded:** at most **3 re-cast rounds**; if the design still can't survive a clean strike, or a finding *dissolves the candidate* (proves the ore was slag), STOP and report the candidate invalidated — that's an honest negative result, not a failure to paper over. The plan is not trusted until the design survives the strike. This is the cold pole; it is what earns the heat back in movement 1.

5. **Blade** — *the finished tool.* Hand the tempered design to **plan mode** (`EnterPlanMode`): translate the surviving design + build order into a concrete, ordered implementation plan the user can approve and start. Blade **hones, it does not re-forge** — it sharpens the already-tempered design into ordered steps; it does NOT run a second cage-match (you temper a blade once, not twice — a redundant full strike can only surface flaws the tempered design already didn't contain). **But honing can expose a crack the mold hid:** if translating the design into an ordered plan reveals a genuine defect — a dependency cycle, an unsafe first step, missing sequencing that invalidates the build order — Blade does **not** silently hone a cracked blade. It returns to **Cast** (and re-Tempers if the fix is structural). The bar for going back is a *real* defect surfaced by sequencing, not a fresh redesign; Blade never introduces new architecture. The light self-review here is: *did Blade drop or distort anything from the tempered design, and does the ordering hold?* The output of `/crucible` is not a prototype and not code — it is a **plan you are excited about that already survived the fire**. Present it, then let Nick's taste own the go/no-go.

## The cast (for Temper)

The `/cage-match` cast, cross-family for genuine adversarial independence: **Maxwell** (Claude), **Kelvin** (Gemini), **Carnot** (Codex/GPT), **Tesla** (Grok). Cross-family is the point — a design lit from four inductive biases finds fatal flaws one model, reviewing its own enthusiasm, will launder past. **Note the temperature flip:** Ore/Heat/Cast run hot (the author, excited); Temper runs cold and adversarial. Do not let the author-instance grade its own homework — the whole value of Temper is that a *different bias* strikes the casting the excited scout poured.

**The persona-fallback does NOT count as a temper (this is where `/crucible` differs from `/ascend`).** Its siblings may fall back to in-context personae when real cross-family agents are unavailable, because they have a *later* cold pole (an overnight compiler). `/crucible` has no later cold pole — Temper IS its only honesty-restorer, and a persona-hat strike is the same thermal body radiating heat back at itself (Kelvin's catch: "the author-instance cannot grade its own homework, even in a different persona-hat"). So: if real cross-family agents are genuinely unavailable, you MAY run personae to sanity-check the *mechanic*, but you must **label the plan `UN-TEMPERED (provisional)` and require a real cross-family strike before any build begins.** A provisional persona-pass is never presented as a survived fire.

## The one discipline: the scout must not grade its own homework

Movement 1 is a *taste* call, and taste at the front is fine and necessary — that's where the joy lives. The danger is letting that same excited voice carry all the way to the plan unchallenged (the ecstasy-spiral echo chamber `/ascend` names — it will forge slag as eagerly as gold). You do **not** fix this by adding a critic to the Ore movement — that kills the heat that makes the scout brave. You fix it at **Temper**: a cold, cross-family adversary strikes the *casting*, not the scout's enthusiasm. Heat proposes; temper disposes; the plan is what's left standing.

## Procedure

1. **Ore.** Scout the codebase + open task list + forward-plan "crazy-ideas" **two ways** — a *forward* scan (what to build) and an *inward* scan (what to extract/combine/simplify/redesign). Reject the slag. Score the survivors on **aliveness × impact** (rubric below), select the peak (tie-break toward impact), and verify it points at a real artifact. If an `$ARGUMENTS` seed was given, let it *bias* the scout (e.g. "inward only", or a domain), not replace the selection.
   - **If `--scout` was passed → STOP HERE, read-only.** Emit the ranked candidate list (each: artifact path/issue URL · one aliveness-evidence bullet · one impact-evidence bullet · scores · confidence) and **forge nothing**: write no files, spawn no agents, mutate no repo, do not deep-research, do not enter plan mode. If no candidate clears the minimum, say so and exit clean. This is the scheduled-sweep contract — cheap eyes on a clock.
   - **Otherwise (full forge): write the pick + the why-this-thrills-me-AND-what-it-changes case + the falsifier to `CRUCIBLE.md`** (placed per *Contracts & guardrails*), then **STOP AT THE CONSENT GATE.**
2. **Consent gate (cage before monster — the skill obeys its own sermon).** The full forge is expensive autonomous work (network research + repo writes + a four-family cage-match + plan mode). Do **not** cross from Ore into Heat unattended. Present the selected ore + the pumped case + the output location, and get Nick's go — **UNLESS** the invocation already named an explicit target that resolves to exactly one verified artifact (then the human already chose; proceed). No blank-cheque forge on a vague invocation.
3. **Heat.** Deep-research the pick (compose `/deep-research` or a background researcher) → `RESEARCH.md`.
4. **Cast.** Write `DESIGN.md`: problem, shape, build order (core-first), tradeoffs, blast-radius + consent spine, **claims-to-falsify + rejected alternatives**, open variables enumerated (no silent `[TODO]`s).
5. **Temper.** Run `/cage-match` over `CRUCIBLE.md` + `RESEARCH.md` + `DESIGN.md`, cross-family, hunting fatal *design* flaws. Fold every finding back (re-Cast) or record it as a named tradeoff (owner + cost + mitigation). **Bounded:** ≤3 re-cast rounds; stop and report *candidate invalidated* if a finding dissolves the ore. If no real cross-family agent is reachable, label the result `UN-TEMPERED (provisional)` — a persona pass is not a strike.
6. **Blade.** `EnterPlanMode` with the tempered design translated into an ordered, approvable plan. If honing exposes a real crack, return to Cast; else present and hand the go/no-go to Nick.

## When the ore is cold (an honest negative result)

If the scout genuinely can't find a candidate that glows — everything is dull, done, or not-yet-ripe — **say so and stop.** A cold crucible is a real, useful result: it means the exciting work isn't in the visible substrate right now, and forging slag into a polished plan just to have shipped something is exactly the failure `/crucible` exists to avoid. Do not manufacture enthusiasm; report the empty melt.

## Cadence — the janitor is crucible on a timer, not a second skill

A recurring "what's rotting?" sweep is **not** a separate skill and its schedule does **not** live inside the scout — a scout that knows what day it is is a capability that grew a trigger (*remove the coupling, don't add a layer*). The janitor is simply **`/crucible --scout` fired by a `/schedule` routine or cron**. Two orthogonal axes, kept apart:

- **Eyes** (what it scans for — rot, duplication, coupling, drift): the Ore movement's *inward scan*. Already in the scout.
- **Clock** (when it runs): a cron/routine that invokes the skill. Lives in the scheduler, never in the movement.

**`--scout` (shallow mode) is READ-ONLY:** run **Ore only** — the two scans + the ranked aliveness × impact list — then **STOP**. It writes no files (not even `CRUCIBLE.md`), spawns no agents, mutates no repo, runs no research, enters no plan mode; its only output is the ranked list to stdout (see the procedure's `--scout` contract). This is what the scheduled sweep uses, and why: an unattended weekly run must **not** burn a full deep-research pass + a four-family cage-match and end in a plan nobody asked for (bounded blast-radius / cost). The cron posts the ranked list; Nick reads it, picks the one that glows, and *then* runs full `/crucible <pick>` on-demand — which crosses the consent gate with a human already at the wheel. Cheap eyes on a clock; expensive forge on a human trigger.

## Contracts & guardrails (the procedure under the metaphor)

The metaphor is the style; these are the load-bearing rules a future instance executes literally. A SKILL.md is a procedure spec, not a manifesto — when the poetry and these rules disagree, these win.

**Scoring rubric (makes `aliveness × impact` operational, not vibes).** Score each survivor **aliveness 0–3** and **impact 0–3**, each backed by **one concrete evidence bullet** (not affect):
- *aliveness* — 0 nobody would build it · 1 mild · 2 a real *oh, of course* · 3 you'd drop other work.
- *impact* — 0 changes nothing · 1 minor convenience · 2 removes a real recurring task / unblocks something stuck · 3 changes what a user or Nick can do.
- **Reject any candidate scoring 0 on *either* axis** (a 3×0 is still slag). Rank by the product. "Close" for the impact tie-break = within 1 product-point. A far-seam novel idea that scores low on impact *only because it's new* gets a framing window before it's cut (protect newborns).

**Artifact placement (no non-existent-target trap).** "The target's dir" is deterministic, not assumed:
- *file/package-backed* ore → the nearest owning package/dir of that file.
- *issue-backed* or *cross-cutting* ore (a refactor spanning N files, a carried-forward idea, a redesign with no single home) → a named proposal dir, `docs/crucible/<slug>/` (or the repo's existing proposals home). Never scatter `CRUCIBLE.md`/`RESEARCH.md`/`DESIGN.md` into an arbitrary file's dir.
- *ambiguous* → **ask before writing.** Placement is a mutation; an unverified destination is the same trap as an unverified ore.

**Composition contract (assertions become invocations with fallbacks).** Each operator has a defined in/out and a behaviour when unavailable — the forge degrades, it doesn't crash:

| Operator | When | In → Out | If unavailable |
|---|---|---|---|
| `/deep-research` | Heat | pick → `RESEARCH.md` | fall back to a background `general-purpose` researcher; if none, do a bounded inline research pass and label Heat *shallow* |
| `/recombine`, `/simplify` | Cast (optional) | ore → a sharper shape | skip; note the operator wasn't run — they refine the ore, they don't silently redirect it (the selected candidate is fixed at the consent gate) |
| `/cage-match` | Temper | 3 hot-phase docs → verdict + findings | no real cross-family agent → `UN-TEMPERED (provisional)`, real strike required before build |
| `EnterPlanMode` | Blade | tempered design → ordered plan | present the plan as prose and say plan mode was unavailable |

**Environment fallback (the scout is not brittle).** The task-list read (`gh issue list -R nickmeinhold/claude-tasks --label "project:<slug>" --state open`) depends on `gh` auth, network, and a `<slug>` = `basename "$(git rev-parse --show-toplevel)"`. If `gh` fails, the slug can't be inferred, or there's no network: **fall back to the in-session TaskList + codebase scan and say the issue-list was skipped** — never invent issues, never block the whole forge on one absent source.

## Composition

- **With /ascend**: `/ascend` is pure heat (no critic, deferred build). Run `/ascend` when you want to know if a *single named ember* is alive; run `/crucible` when you want the engine to *find* the ember itself and carry it all the way to a vetted plan. `/crucible`'s Ore movement is a `/ascend`-flavored heating of the chosen candidate; its Temper is a `/cage-match`. It recombines both on the anchor *"forge excitement into a plan without letting the excitement lie."*
- **With /cage-match**: `/crucible` *is* a caller of `/cage-match` — pointed at a design doc rather than a PR diff. The adversaries hunt design flaws (missing failure modes, simpler alternatives, uncounted blast-radius), not code bugs.
- **With /deep-research**: the Heat movement is a `/deep-research` run scoped to the chosen candidate.
- **With plan mode**: `/crucible` terminates *into* plan mode — it is the pre-flight that makes the plan worth approving. What plan mode receives has already been researched and tempered.
- **With /spiral-review**: the inverse arc's far end. Once the plan is built, `/spiral-review` cools the shipped thing's review findings into contracts. `/crucible` forges the plan; `/spiral-review` files the fixes.
